Our Story

We Didn't Just Build
a Company.

We Built a Mission.

It began in the Masai Mara in 2006 — a young Kenyan naturalist named David Ochieng, watching a family of lions from the back of a Land Rover, struck by the realisation that most safari operators were showing tourists through Africa rather than showing them Africa itself.

Two years later, with a single vehicle, two guides, and an unshakeable belief in honest storytelling, he founded SUCCESSFUL LUXURY SAFARIS in Nairobi. The idea was simple: small groups, deep knowledge, and a genuine partnership with the communities and ecosystems at the heart of each journey.

Today, we operate across five countries with 38 specialist guides and a team that spans three continents. But the core promise has never changed: every guest leaves knowing Africa differently — and leaving Africa a little better than they found it.

We are family-run, conservation-funded, and fiercely independent. We have never compromised quality for volume, and we never will.

What We Believe

Our Core Values

The principles that guide every decision we make — from the packages we design to the partners we choose

🦁
Wildlife First
Every itinerary is designed around animal welfare and natural behaviour. We never rush encounters, chase animals, or prioritise spectacle over respect.
🤝
Community Partnership
We employ local guides, stay in community-owned lodges, and reinvest directly into the villages and conservancies that protect the land we visit.
🔍
Radical Honesty
We tell you exactly what to expect — the dust, the distances, the early mornings. No overpromising. If a package isn't right for you, we'll say so.
🌍
Low Impact Travel
Carbon-offset flights, solar-powered camps, reusable water systems, and no single-use plastics across all our operations since 2018.
✦
Obsessive Quality
We operate at a scale that lets us personally vet every vehicle, lodge, and guide we recommend. If we wouldn't send our own family there, we won't send you.
📚
Deep Knowledge
Our guides hold degrees in ecology, conservation, and zoology. Minimum five years field experience before leading a guest. We don't cut corners on expertise.
