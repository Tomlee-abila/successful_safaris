What You'll See

Kenya's Iconic Wildlife

Every game drive is different. These are the encounters that define a Kenya safari.

Big Five
African Lion
Panthera leo
The Masai Mara holds some of Africa's densest lion populations. Pride interactions — hunts, cub play, territorial confrontations — are frequent and extraordinary.
Best: Masai Mara · Jul – Oct
Spectacle
Wildebeest Migration
Connochaetes taurinus
Over 1.5 million wildebeest and 300,000 zebra crossing the Mara River in a primal, thundering spectacle. The world's greatest wildlife event.
Best: Masai Mara · Jul – Oct
Big Five
African Elephant
Loxodonta africana
Amboseli's elephants are the most studied on Earth. Watch vast matriarchal herds move in silence against the backdrop of Kilimanjaro.
Best: Amboseli · All Year
Rare
Reticulated Giraffe
Giraffa reticulata
One of Kenya's Northern Specials, found only in Samburu and Laikipia. Larger patches and a more vivid pattern than the common Masai giraffe.
Best: Samburu · All Year
Endangered
Black Rhinoceros
Diceros bicornis
Laikipia Plateau hosts Kenya's densest black rhino population outside national parks. Night tracking safaris offer heart-stopping encounters at close range.
Best: Laikipia · All Year
Iconic
Cheetah
Acinonyx jubatus
The open short-grass plains of the Mara Triangle are among the best places on Earth to witness a cheetah hunt at full sprint — 112 km/h of pure grace.
Best: Masai Mara · Jun – Oct
Big Five
Cape Buffalo
Syncerus caffer
Massive herds roam Tsavo and the Mara, while lone dagga boys lurk in thick bush — unpredictable and magnetic in equal measure.
Best: Tsavo · All Year
Nocturnal
Leopard
Panthera pardus
Solitary and elusive. The Mara's fig trees and Laikipia's rocky outcrops are prime locations for sightings of the most beautiful of Africa's cats.
Best: Masai Mara · Year-round

The Migration Season
July — October

The wildebeest river crossings are at their dramatic peak. Short dry grass means superb visibility for predator action. This is Kenya at its most spectacular — and most sought-after.

Book 12–18 months in advance
🌿
The Shoulder Season
January — February · June · December

Excellent game viewing with fewer visitors. January–February is calving season in the Mara — newborn wildebeest and the predator activity that follows is unforgettable.

Best value for money
🌧️
The Green Season
March — May · November

Long rains transform Kenya into vivid emerald. Dramatic skies, abundant birdlife, newborn animals everywhere. Fewer tourists, lower rates, and a genuine feeling of wilderness solitude.

Common Questions

Kenya Safari FAQ

Answers to the questions our guests ask most before their first Kenya adventure.

When is the best time to see the Great Migration in Kenya? +
The Mara River crossings — the most dramatic part of the migration — typically occur between late July and early October, though exact timing varies year to year depending on rainfall patterns in Tanzania's Serengeti. July–August tends to see the highest crossing activity. We monitor the movement in real-time and update itineraries accordingly.
Do I need a visa to enter Kenya? +
Kenya introduced the Electronic Travel Authorisation (eTA) in January 2024, replacing the old visa system. Most nationalities — including all EU and US citizens — apply online at etakenya.go.ke before travel. The eTA currently costs $30 USD and is typically approved within 72 hours. Our team provides full pre-departure documentation support.
Are yellow fever vaccinations required for Kenya? +
Kenya does not require yellow fever vaccination for entry unless you are arriving from a yellow fever endemic country. However, we strongly recommend consulting a travel health clinic 6–8 weeks before departure. Recommended vaccinations typically include Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, and up-to-date Tetanus. Malaria prophylaxis is advised for all wildlife areas.
What is the typical accommodation on a Kenya safari? +
Most of our Kenya packages use luxury tented camps — permanent structures with en-suite bathrooms, king beds, and all modern amenities, but with canvas walls that bring you close to nature. Standards range from premier (from $350/pp/night all-inclusive) to ultra-luxury (from $1,200/pp/night). We also use lodges and private villa options. All properties are personally vetted.
How do I get between parks in Kenya? +
Inter-park transfers in Kenya are typically by scheduled or charter light aircraft — bush flights on Cessna Caravans from airstrips within the parks. Flying reduces long road transfers and maximises your time on game drives. For shorter distances (e.g., Nairobi to Amboseli), road transfers in private 4×4 vehicles are used. All logistics are pre-arranged by our operations team.
Is Kenya safe for tourists? +
Kenya's wildlife areas and national parks are extremely safe. Our guides are licensed by the Kenya Professional Safari Guides Association (KPSGA) and operate to strict safety protocols. Nairobi has areas that require the same common-sense precautions as any major city, and our team provides thorough pre-departure safety briefings. We maintain 24/7 on-ground support throughout your journey.
What currency is used in Kenya? +
Kenya's currency is the Kenyan Shilling (KES). However, USD is widely accepted in safari lodges, camps, and tourist areas. We recommend carrying USD for tips, curio purchases, and small expenses. Most lodge extras and park fees can also be settled by card. Our pre-departure pack includes a comprehensive money and tipping guide.
Can you build a bespoke Kenya itinerary for us? +
Absolutely — bespoke design is our core service. Whether you want a family itinerary with young children, a honeymoon with ultra-romantic camps, a photography safari with specialist equipment, or a multi-country circuit, we design every detail around you. Use the contact form to share your vision and we'll respond within 24 hours with a tailored proposal.
